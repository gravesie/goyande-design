The idea

A practical agentic AI agency

Goyande helps companies get started with AI in a practical way and benefit from better automation and efficiency. We put human-like judgement into autonomous agents, so processes get smarter rather than just faster.

The name draws on two Apache words, wise and human. We treat those as the two values behind the company, not just a translation of the name.

Wise
Judgement over raw automation. Agents that reason about the work and make good calls, not just fast ones.
Human
Built around the people who use them. Plain language, humans kept in the loop, the final say stays with you.

These two values sit behind the messaging and copy. They are not a strapline and not part of the logo; they guide how Goyande talks and what it promises.

Clear space & minimum size

Give it room

Keep clear space around the logo equal to the height of one petal cluster (roughly the height of the mark's centre petal). Nothing else sits inside that margin. Do not place the logo on a busy photo without a solid holding shape behind it.

Minimum sizes.

Full lockup: no smaller than 120px wide on screen, or 28mm in print.

Mark on its own: no smaller than 24px. Below 24px, use the simplified favicon (three petals), which drops the two faint outer petals so it stays clean.

Colour

Palette

Deep Navy carries the brand and builds the corporate trust an enterprise buyer expects. Agent Teal is the single accent: growth, action, the live part of the system. Use teal sparingly, on the one thing you want noticed. Steel is for secondary text. Paper is the off-white background.

Impact surface

The brand gradient

A teal-to-navy gradient for high-impact moments: the website hero, a LinkedIn banner, social and link-preview cards, a deck's title and divider slides, an app splash. It is a background surface only. It is never applied to the mark, and the mark on top stays flat white.

Rules.

  • Green sweeps in from the top-left corner; text and the wordmark always sit over the navy side.
  • The lotus on the gradient is white at graded opacity (outer 40%, inner 85%, centre 100%) so the petals stay distinct.
  • Balanced is the default. Use Vivid only where there is no text on the surface, such as an app splash or a divider.
  • Use the gradient sparingly. It is the exception that makes the navy look richer, not a default background. Keep it off body text and one-colour print.

Typography

Manrope

One family does everything: Manrope. It is free and open-source (SIL Open Font Licence), geometric enough to feel technical, humanist enough to feel approachable. The wordmark is set lowercase in SemiBold with tight tracking.

Voice

Straplines

Two lines, two jobs. Use the primary line as the brand signature under the logo and on the website hero. Use the secondary line where a buyer needs to know exactly what you do in plain terms, such as the LinkedIn tagline, an email footer or a meta description.

Primary · brand line
Go beyond automation.

Secondary · explainer
Practical AI. Automated efficiency.

Tells a sceptical operations director what they get. No hype. For bios and descriptions.

Misuse

What not to do

Don't stretch
Don't recolour
Don't rotate
Don't put the navy mark on the gradient

Also: do not add drop shadows or outlines to the mark, do not crowd it, and do not rebuild it in a different typeface. The gradient is a background surface with a flat white mark on top, never applied to the mark itself.

Tone of voice

How Goyande sounds

  • Plain English. Say what the agent does and what the client gets. No jargon walls.
  • Practical over futuristic. Lead with the outcome (hours saved, errors cut), not the technology.
  • Calm and certain. The audience is wary of AI hype, so understatement builds more trust than big claims.
  • Specific. Name the process, the tool, the result. Avoid vague "transformation" language.
  • Human. The brand's values are wise and human; the writing should sound like a sharp colleague, not a chatbot.
